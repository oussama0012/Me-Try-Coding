Why Do You Need Strong Passwords?

Think of passwords like the locks on your house. Would you use a flimsy lock made of paper? Your digital life deserves better protection than "123456" or "password".

Weak passwords are like leaving your front door wide open with a sign saying "Rob me please!" Strong passwords are your first line of defense against:

  • Brute force attacks (computers trying millions of combinations)
  • Dictionary attacks (using common words and phrases)
  • Social engineering (hackers guessing based on your personal info)
  • Data breaches (when one weak password compromises everything)

Password Best Practices (The Boring But Important Stuff)

  • Use unique passwords everywhere – Don't recycle passwords like old socks
  • Enable two-factor authentication – Even if they crack your password, they still can't get in
  • Use a password manager – Store all those unique passwords safely
  • Change compromised passwords immediately – If a site gets hacked, update that password ASAP
  • Don't share passwords – Not even with your bestie

Common Password Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using personal information (birthdays, names, addresses)
  • Dictionary words or common phrases
  • Simple patterns (123456, qwerty, abc123)
  • Reusing passwords across multiple sites
  • Writing passwords on sticky notes
  • Using the same password with slight variations
