What Is WHOIS Information?

WHOIS data is like the property records of the internet. It tells you who owns and manages IP addresses, including registration dates, network ranges, and contact details for the organizations responsible.

Two kinds of IP addresses can be looked up:

  • IPv4 ‚Äì the classic format (looks like 123.45.67.89)
  • IPv6 ‚Äì the newer, longer format (looks like 2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334)

Every device has an IP, and every IP is registered to an organization in the WHOIS database.

Let's be realistic about WHOIS data limitations:

  • Some registrants use privacy services to mask their details
  • Different regional registries provide varying levels of detail
  • Some historical allocation information may be limited
  • WHOIS databases are independently maintained by each registry

Understanding WHOIS Privacy

WHOIS data is publicly available by design - it's meant to provide transparency about who operates internet resources. That said, the level of detail varies by registry policies. Some organizations use privacy services for domains but cannot fully shield their details for IP allocations.
